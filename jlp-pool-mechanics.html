Jupiter Perpetuals — Liquidity Pool

How JLP Works

JLP is the liquidity pool backing Jupiter's perpetuals exchange. Traders trade against the pool. Fees flow in and compound into token price automatically — no claiming, no reinvesting.

02 — core mechanics
01
Fees raise price, not supply
75% of all perps trading fees flow into the pool AUM without minting new JLP. This makes the token price rise for all existing holders automatically.
02
New entrants don't dilute you
New LPs mint JLP at the current (higher) virtual price. They don't receive past fee accrual — they start earning from their entry price forward.
03
Auto-compounding NAV
Yield compounds directly into the token price. No claiming, no restaking. Your JLP is just worth more each day as fees accumulate.
04
Traders are the risk
The pool is the counterparty to every trade. Winning traders take from the pool; losing traders add to it. Net trader PnL is the main source of NAV volatility.
03 — price mechanics

Virtual Price vs Market Price

Virtual Price
Pool AUM ÷ JLP Supply
The true NAV. Rises as fees flow in, falls if traders win big.
Market Price
Secondary DEX price
What JLP trades for on spot markets. Usually tracks virtual price closely.
Redemption Rule
max(virtual, market)
If market price drops below virtual price, you always redeem at virtual. Built-in floor.
KV Net Yield
JLP yield − hedge cost
KV earns the JLP fee yield while hedging out SOL/BTC/ETH price exposure. What remains is near-pure USDC yield.
04 — why jupiter caps the pool

The AUM Cap: A Trading Venue Decision, Not an LP Decision

  • 01
    Fees scale with volume, not AUM Jupiter earns 25% of trading fees. Fee dollars = volume × rate. Pool size doesn't enter the equation. Doubling AUM without doubling volume halves LP yield — Jupiter earns the same, but the product looks worse.
  • 02
    Utilization must stay high Capital not deployed as collateral backing open positions earns nothing. Unlimited inflows would crater utilization, meaning most LP capital just sits idle dragging down the blended APY.
  • 03
    Jupiter can't overpromise liquidity The pool is the counterparty. More capital = more potential liability if traders win. If OI-to-pool ratio gets too high, one bad run of winning traders could seriously damage the pool. The cap keeps risk ratios healthy and ensures Jupiter can always honor positions.
  • 04
    The cap grows as volume scales Jupiter raises the cap deliberately as organic trading demand increases. It's a pacing mechanism — "we have confidence capital will follow demand" — not a permanent ceiling. Growing the cap is a signal of product maturity, not LP generosity.
05 — risk to keyvault

⚠ Yield Compression Risk

KV's alpha = JLP yield − hedging costs. If Jupiter uncaps the pool and capital floods in without proportional volume growth, JLP yield compresses — but hedge costs (funding rates, borrow) don't follow. The spread tightens or disappears.

The mitigation: the cross-platform strategy routes to whichever venue has the best utilization at any given time, reducing dependence on JLP alone. But JLP remains the core yield engine — this is a real, slow-moving risk worth monitoring and disclosing in the PPM.
