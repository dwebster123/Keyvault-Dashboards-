1. Executive Summary

KeyVault operates a delta-neutral hedged vault strategy on the Solana blockchain, utilizing Jupiter Liquidity Provider (JLP) tokens as its core asset with perpetual futures hedges executed on Drift Protocol. The fund is designed to generate yield while minimizing directional market exposure.

Key security principles:

  • Non-custodial architecture. All investor assets reside in an on-chain Drift Protocol vault smart contract — not in KeyVault's bank accounts, not on centralized exchanges, and not under any individual's direct control.
  • On-chain verifiability. Any investor can independently verify vault holdings at any time via Solana block explorers. The vault address is public: G3RT2wdEYCphzcvXEHb8u4Yc4ZRscsQ1KRYywdBjgUZp.
  • Separation of trading and withdrawal. The vault's smart contract design permits fund managers to execute trades but not to withdraw funds to personal wallets. Only depositors can withdraw their own funds.
  • Audited infrastructure. Drift Protocol has been audited by Trail of Bits, Neodyme, and OtterSec — three of the industry's most respected security firms.

This document provides a comprehensive overview of KeyVault's security posture, risk factors, and liability framework. It is intended to give investors the information necessary to evaluate the fund's protective measures and residual risks.

2. Custody Architecture

2.1 Non-Custodial Model

KeyVault does not take custody of investor funds in the traditional sense. Assets are deposited directly into a Drift Protocol vault smart contract deployed on the Solana blockchain. This means:

  • No bank accounts hold investor capital. Funds exist on-chain at all times.
  • No single individual can move funds. The smart contract enforces all deposit, trading, and withdrawal rules programmatically.
  • Fund managers are delegates, not custodians. The vault manager has delegated trading authority — the ability to open and close positions — but the smart contract prohibits withdrawals to any wallet other than the original depositor's.

2.3 Vault Mechanics

Drift Vaults are purpose-built smart contracts for managed trading strategies. The architecture enforces the following constraints:

  1. Deposits go directly from the investor's wallet to the vault's on-chain account.
  2. The vault manager (delegate) can execute trades on Drift — long/short perpetuals, spot swaps — but cannot withdraw funds from the vault to any external wallet.
  3. Withdrawals are initiated by depositors only. A redemption period applies, during which the vault manager may need to unwind positions to free liquidity.
  4. If a withdrawal request would breach margin requirements, Drift's protocol-level liquidation mechanism activates — a permissioned liquidator temporarily assumes control to close positions in reduce-only mode, ensuring the withdrawal can be processed.
  5. Vault profits are distributed proportionally based on each depositor's share of the vault.

2.4 On-Chain Transparency

All vault activity is recorded on the Solana blockchain and is publicly verifiable:

  • Vault address: G3RT2wdEYCphzcvXEHb8u4Yc4ZRscsQ1KRYywdBjgUZp
  • Verification tools: Solscan, Solana Explorer, Drift UI
  • What you can verify: Total vault value, current positions, deposit/withdrawal history, trading activity

This level of transparency is fundamentally different from traditional fund structures, where investors rely on periodic statements and third-party audits to verify holdings.

2.5 No Commingling

Investor funds are held in a dedicated Drift vault. They are not mixed with:

  • KeyVault principals' personal funds
  • Other fund strategies or vault products
  • Operating capital or management fees (until distributed per fund terms)

3.3 Oracle Risk

Drift Protocol relies on decentralized price oracles for position pricing and liquidation triggers:

  • Pyth Network — primary oracle, providing high-frequency price feeds from institutional market makers
  • Switchboard — secondary/backup oracle infrastructure

Oracle risk includes potential price manipulation, stale feeds during network congestion, and discrepancies between oracle prices and actual market prices. Drift mitigates this through multi-oracle validation and circuit breakers.

3.4 Mitigation

  • Delta-neutral hedging reduces directional price exposure. The strategy holds JLP (long exposure to the basket) while shorting equivalent notional value via Drift perpetual futures, targeting market-neutral returns from yield alone.
  • Health monitoring — vault leverage, margin ratios, and liquidation thresholds are monitored continuously.
  • Liquidation buffers — positions are sized to maintain comfortable distance from liquidation prices, even during volatile conditions.

4. Operational Security

4.1 Vault Management

  • Sub-manager: The delegated trading manager operates on Drift Protocol. They execute the delta-neutral strategy — maintaining JLP positions and corresponding perpetual hedges.
  • Key security: Vault management keys are secured by the vault manager following institutional key management practices. The delegate key permits trading only; it cannot withdraw funds.

4.2 Monitoring

  • 24/7 automated monitoring of vault health metrics including leverage ratio, margin utilization, liquidation prices, and hedge ratios.
  • Alert systems trigger notifications when key metrics deviate from target ranges, enabling rapid response before issues escalate.
  • Hedge rebalancing occurs as needed to maintain delta neutrality as JLP composition and market conditions shift.

4.3 Daily NAV Stamps

  • Official NAV (Net Asset Value) calculation is performed daily at 5:00 PM EST.
  • NAV Consulting serves as the fund's independent administrator, providing third-party verification of share pricing.
  • Auditable trail: Each daily NAV stamp creates a timestamped, verifiable record of fund performance, ensuring consistency and accountability.

4.4 Emergency Procedures

  • Position unwinding protocols: In adverse scenarios, the vault manager can rapidly close positions to reduce exposure and free liquidity.
  • Kill switches: Emergency procedures exist to halt active trading and move the vault to a defensive posture (fully hedged or cash-equivalent).
  • Communication chain: Fund principals (Deven Webster, Brett Nielsen, JD Beck) maintain direct communication channels with the vault manager for rapid decision-making.

5. Counterparty Exposure

5.1 Solana Network Risk

Solana has experienced network congestion and outages historically. During a Solana outage:

  • Positions are preserved. Smart contract state does not change during downtime — no positions are liquidated and no funds are lost.
  • Trading is paused. No new trades can be executed, which means hedges cannot be rebalanced until the network resumes.
  • Risk window: Extended outages during extreme market volatility could result in unhedged exposure if the network recovers to significantly different prices.

5.2 Drift Protocol Risk

  • Protocol solvency: Drift maintains an insurance fund to cover socialized losses from liquidation shortfalls. However, a catastrophic event (protocol exploit, mass liquidation cascade) could exceed insurance fund capacity.
  • Protocol upgrades: Drift's program can be upgraded by its governance. Upgrade risk is mitigated by audit requirements and governance processes, but it exists.
  • Operational risk: Drift's keeper/liquidator bot infrastructure must remain functional for the protocol to operate correctly.

5.3 Jupiter / JLP Risk

  • LP token depeg: JLP's value is derived from the underlying basket of assets plus accrued fees. If Jupiter's perpetuals exchange experienced a catastrophic event (e.g., mass trader profits against the pool, exploit), JLP's value could decrease substantially.
  • Composition risk: JLP holds volatile assets (SOL, BTC, ETH). While KeyVault hedges this exposure, the hedge relies on Drift perpetual futures pricing tracking spot pricing — basis risk exists.

5.4 Stablecoin Risk

  • USDC (issued by Circle) comprises a significant portion of the JLP pool and is used for margin on Drift.
  • Circle's track record: Circle maintains attestations of USDC reserves, is regulated as a money transmitter, and survived the March 2023 SVB depeg event (recovering to peg within days). USDC briefly traded at $0.87 during that event.
  • Residual risk: A sustained USDC depeg would impact both the JLP basket value and margin collateral on Drift.

Liquidity Risk — A Real Example

On February 6, 2026, a $520,000 withdrawal was processed while the vault was operating at 2.4x leverage. The position unwinding required to fulfill the redemption resulted in approximately 8% execution drag (slippage and market impact). This event demonstrates that:

  1. Large withdrawals during elevated leverage periods have measurable costs.
  2. The fund honors redemptions — capital is never locked indefinitely.
  3. Investors should be aware that redemption timing can impact realized returns.

This transparency is intentional. We believe investors are better served by understanding real-world mechanics than by theoretical projections.

7. Insurance & Liability

7.1 Drift Insurance Fund

Drift Protocol maintains an insurance fund sourced from protocol revenue. This fund covers socialized losses from liquidation shortfalls (when a liquidation does not fully cover a position's losses). The insurance fund provides a backstop but has a finite balance and would not cover a catastrophic protocol exploit.

7.2 No FDIC / SIPC Coverage

KeyVault is not a bank or registered broker-dealer. Investor deposits are not insured by the FDIC, SIPC, or any government agency. If assets are lost due to smart contract exploit, protocol failure, or other covered risk factors, there is no government backstop.

7.3 Fund Liability Structure

  • KeyVault is structured as a limited partnership. Investor liability is limited to their invested capital.
  • Fund principals and the general partner bear management responsibility under the terms of the partnership agreement.
  • The fund's legal structure is overseen by Brian Forman of Morrison Cohen LLP.

7.4 Professional Liability Insurance

9. Disaster Recovery

9.1 Vault Manager Goes Offline

If the delegated vault manager becomes unable to manage the vault:

  1. Existing positions remain open on Drift — the smart contract continues to hold and margin positions.
  2. Fund principals (Deven Webster, Brett Nielsen, JD Beck) can appoint a replacement delegate or directly manage the vault's delegate key.
  3. Worst case: Positions can be unwound and the vault can be moved to a cash/stablecoin position until a replacement manager is appointed.
  4. Investor withdrawals can still be processed — the vault's deposit/withdrawal mechanics are independent of the trading delegate.

9.2 Drift Protocol Exploit

In the event of a Drift Protocol exploit:

  1. Drift's insurance fund would be the first line of defense for covered losses.
  2. If the exploit affects the vault program itself, recovery depends on the nature and scope of the exploit. Drift's team and governance would coordinate a response.
  3. KeyVault maintains records of all depositor shares, NAV history, and position snapshots to support potential claims or recovery processes.

9.3 Emergency Withdrawal Procedures

  • Depositors can initiate withdrawals directly through the Drift vault interface at any time.
  • During extreme market conditions, a redemption period applies to allow orderly position unwinding.
  • Fund principals maintain the ability to communicate directly with investors via established channels if vault access is disrupted.

9.4 Data Backup & Continuity

  • On-chain data (positions, balances, transaction history) is inherently preserved on the Solana blockchain.
  • Off-chain records (NAV calculations, investor communications, compliance documentation) are maintained by NAV Consulting and fund principals with appropriate backup procedures.
  • Legal documentation is maintained by Morrison Cohen LLP.
