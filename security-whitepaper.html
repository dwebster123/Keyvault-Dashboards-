1. Executive Summary

KeyVault operates a delta-neutral JLP strategy on Solana using a layered infrastructure stack built around institutional MPC custody and on-chain vault automation. The current design is intended to preserve transparency, reduce single-key risk, and support disciplined deployment across approved DeFi venues while minimizing directional market exposure.

Key security principles:

  • Layered custody and execution. Cobo provides the MPC custody and approval layer, while Voltr provides the on-chain vault framework that handles deposits, share accounting, and strategy allocations.
  • Policy-controlled access. MPC policies, role separation, and vault-level permissions are intended to limit who can approve actions, configure integrations, or move capital between strategies.
  • On-chain transparency. Positions, balances, and wallet activity remain observable on Solana, giving investors a materially higher degree of verifiability than a traditional opaque fund structure.
  • Multi-protocol resilience. The strategy is designed around approved integrations rather than reliance on a single venue, reducing concentration in one execution path.

This document provides a practical overview of KeyVault's custody design, operational controls, infrastructure dependencies, and residual risks. It is intended to help investors evaluate both the protective measures in place and the risks that remain inherent to digital asset strategies.

2. Custody & Access Stack

2.1 Cobo MPC Custody

KeyVault uses Cobo MPC as the custody and transaction-approval layer for strategy operations. Multi-party computation replaces dependence on a single private key with distributed signing shares and policy controls, reducing single-key compromise risk while allowing institution-style governance over approvals.

  • Policy-controlled approvals. Signing workflows can be gated by internal rules, user roles, and approval thresholds rather than a single operator's wallet.
  • Operational segregation. The custody layer is distinct from the strategy logic layer, helping separate key management from portfolio allocation logic.
  • Reduced key exposure. Private-key material is not concentrated in one hot wallet or one person's device.

2.2 Voltr Vault Architecture

KeyVault's strategy logic is structured around a Voltr vault on Solana. Voltr vaults accept deposits, track user shares, and allocate capital into initialized strategies through standardized adaptors.

2.3 Role Separation & Withdrawal Controls

Voltr's owner documentation emphasizes role separation between admin and manager keys. In practical terms, the configuration authority and the allocation authority should not be concentrated in the same place without explicit approval controls.

  • Admin role: updates vault configuration, initializes strategies, and manages adaptor permissions.
  • Manager role: allocates vault funds between approved strategies and rebalances positions.
  • Investor flow: deposits move into the vault, funds can be deployed into strategy accounts, and withdrawals are serviced from vault liquidity or by pulling assets back from strategies.

The operating objective is to constrain strategy personnel to portfolio management functions rather than unrestricted off-platform withdrawal authority.

2.4 Security & Infrastructure

The current stack is designed around three separate control surfaces:

  • Custody & deployment: institutional MPC custody with policy-controlled signing and automated deployment support.
  • Smart contract architecture: modular on-chain vault infrastructure via Voltr, allowing managed strategies to be deployed with transparent accounting and standardized adaptors.
  • Protocol & operational security: integrations are selected for liquidity, maturity, and security track record, while monitoring and alerting are intended to catch execution anomalies early.

2.5 No Commingling

Investor funds are intended to remain separated from:

  • KeyVault principals' personal funds
  • Other fund strategies or unrelated operating accounts
  • Management-fee or performance-fee distributions before they are properly accrued and transferred under fund terms

3. Smart Contract & Technology Risk

3.1 Voltr Framework

Voltr is the smart contract framework underlying the vault structure. According to Voltr's security documentation, the vault and adaptor programs have passed Sec3 X-RAY scans, with additional formal audits expected as the protocol matures. This is useful assurance, but it is not a guarantee against vulnerabilities.

  • Vault program: handles deposits, withdrawals, fee accounting, and share tracking.
  • Adaptor layer: connects the vault to external DeFi protocols.
  • Residual risk: bugs may exist in the vault, adaptors, allocation logic, or external integrations.

3.2 JLP Exposure

JLP remains the core exposure in the strategy. It is a yield-bearing liquidity position whose economics depend on underlying asset composition, trading activity, liquidity conditions, and protocol-level performance. Even in a hedged structure, JLP introduces basis, liquidity, and smart contract risk.

  • Underlying composition risk: the basket includes volatile assets and stablecoins whose weights change over time.
  • Liquidity-pool risk: trader P&L, fee conditions, and venue stress can affect JLP performance.
  • Protocol risk: bugs or failures in the underlying JLP venue could impair capital.

3.3 Multi-Protocol Execution Risk

A multi-venue strategy reduces single-venue dependence, but it also expands the surface area for operational and technical failure. Each approved integration adds its own contracts, oracle assumptions, liquidity profile, and governance risk.

  • Adaptor risk: the routing layer must correctly interface with each venue.
  • Execution risk: periods of volatility can create slippage, temporary hedge imprecision, or delayed rebalancing.
  • Liquidity risk: the best venue in normal conditions may not remain the best venue during a stress event.

3.4 Mitigation Framework

  • Role separation between custody, configuration, and allocation functions reduces concentration of operational authority.
  • Allocation caps and venue selection standards are intended to prevent overconcentration in a single integration.
  • Monitoring and alerts support rapid response if utilization, position health, or execution quality deviates from target ranges.
  • Dynamic rebalancing aims to preserve a market-neutral profile while keeping enough liquidity available for orderly operations.

4. Operational Security

4.1 Access Control & Key Management

  • MPC governance: signing authority is distributed, with policy controls intended to reduce reliance on any single device, key, or operator.
  • Least-privilege design: administrative, allocation, and reporting responsibilities are separated wherever practical.
  • Approval discipline: sensitive actions should require policy checks and documented authorization paths rather than informal wallet access.

4.2 Monitoring

  • 24/7 automated monitoring of vault health metrics including deployed vs. idle capital, hedge coverage, utilization, and unusual wallet or strategy activity.
  • Alert systems are intended to trigger when key metrics deviate from target ranges, enabling rapid response before issues escalate.
  • Rebalancing procedures are designed to maintain the intended risk profile as market conditions and venue economics change.

4.3 Daily NAV Stamps

  • Official NAV (Net Asset Value) calculation is performed daily at 5:00 PM EST.
  • NAV Consulting serves as the fund's independent administrator, providing third-party verification of share pricing.
  • Auditable trail: Each daily NAV stamp creates a timestamped, verifiable record of fund performance, ensuring consistency and accountability.

4.4 Emergency Procedures

  • Position unwinding protocols: in adverse scenarios, allocations can be reduced and positions can be unwound to lower risk and free liquidity.
  • Kill switches and pauses: emergency procedures exist to halt new allocations and move the strategy to a more defensive posture when required.
  • Communication chain: Fund principals (Deven Webster, Brett Nielsen, JD Beck) maintain direct communication channels with the vault manager for rapid decision-making.

5. Counterparty & Infrastructure Exposure

5.1 Solana Network Risk

Solana has experienced network congestion and outages historically. During a Solana outage:

  • Positions are preserved on-chain. State remains recorded, but normal operations may pause.
  • Rebalancing and withdrawals may be delayed. If assets are deployed or hedges need adjustment, operations may not occur until the network resumes.
  • Risk window: extended outages during major volatility can create temporary mismatch between intended and actual risk posture.

5.2 MPC Custody Provider Risk

  • Operational dependency: the strategy relies on Cobo for custody workflows, policy enforcement, and signer infrastructure.
  • Service disruption risk: a provider outage, compliance freeze, or integration incident could delay operations even if underlying assets remain on-chain.
  • Residual dependency risk: MPC reduces single-key exposure but does not eliminate third-party operational dependency.

5.3 Integrated Protocol Risk

  • Integration failure: any venue used for borrowing, hedging, liquidity deployment, or settlement may suffer outages, oracle problems, governance changes, or exploit events.
  • Routing complexity: a diversified strategy is more resilient than a single-venue design, but it also creates more moving parts that must function correctly together.
  • JLP-specific risk: if the underlying JLP venue or market structure experiences severe stress, the strategy can be affected even if custody and routing layers function as designed.

5.4 Stablecoin & Settlement Risk

  • USDC and other settlement assets remain a meaningful risk surface for DeFi strategies.
  • Issuer or market stress can impair liquidity, collateral value, or withdrawal timing.
  • Residual risk: a severe depeg or settlement disruption could affect both deployed capital and idle liquidity reserves.

7. Insurance & Liability

7.1 No FDIC / SIPC Coverage

KeyVault is not a bank or registered broker-dealer. Investor deposits are not insured by the FDIC, SIPC, or any government agency. If assets are lost due to smart contract exploit, protocol failure, or other covered risk factors, there is no government backstop.

7.2 Third-Party Provider Limitations

Cobo, Voltr, and any integrated DeFi venues are infrastructure providers, not guarantees against loss. Any vendor-side insurance, recovery framework, or security representation may be limited in scope, unavailable to fund investors directly, or insufficient in a severe loss scenario.

7.3 Fund Liability Structure

  • KeyVault is structured as a limited partnership. Investor liability is limited to their invested capital.
  • Fund principals and the general partner bear management responsibility under the terms of the partnership agreement.
  • The fund's legal structure is overseen by Brian Forman of Morrison Cohen LLP.

7.4 Professional Liability Insurance

Professional liability (errors & omissions) insurance coverage for fund operations is being evaluated. Investors will be notified if and when such coverage is secured.

9. Disaster Recovery

9.1 Vault Manager Goes Offline

If the delegated manager becomes unable to operate the strategy:

  1. Existing on-chain positions remain where they are until they are actively adjusted, unwound, or transferred under approved procedures.
  2. Fund principals (Deven Webster, Brett Nielsen, JD Beck) can appoint a replacement operator or suspend new allocations while control is re-established.
  3. Worst case: positions can be reduced and the strategy can be moved to a more defensive posture while a replacement operating path is established.
  4. Withdrawals may still be processed subject to available idle liquidity and the time required to unwind deployed capital.

9.2 Custody Provider Outage

In the event of a material outage or disruption involving the MPC custody layer:

  1. Underlying on-chain positions do not disappear solely because the custody provider is impaired.
  2. Operations may slow materially while failover, recovery, or emergency access procedures are executed.
  3. KeyVault maintains records of depositor balances, NAV history, and position snapshots to support operational recovery and investor communication.

9.3 Protocol Exploit or Strategy Halt

  • New allocations can be halted while exposure is assessed.
  • Deployed capital can be pulled back from strategies as conditions permit, with priority given to preserving capital and restoring operational clarity.
  • Fund principals maintain the ability to communicate directly with investors if normal interfaces or dashboards are disrupted.

9.4 Data Backup & Continuity

  • On-chain data (positions, balances, transaction history) is inherently preserved on Solana.
  • Off-chain records (NAV calculations, investor communications, compliance documentation) are maintained by NAV Consulting and fund principals with appropriate backup procedures.
  • Legal documentation is maintained by Morrison Cohen LLP.
