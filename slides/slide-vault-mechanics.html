How The Vault Works
The complete flow — from your dollar to yield and back
1
Investor Deposits USD
You wire US dollars into the fund. We convert those dollars into USDC — a dollar-pegged stablecoin. 1 USDC = $1, always.
Think of it as putting your dollars on-chain. Same value, now programmable.
2
USDC Enters the Vault
The USDC is deposited into a decentralized vault — a smart contract with predefined rules written in code. No middleman. The rules govern exactly how the money is deployed.
3
Vault Buys JLP
The vault converts USDC into JLP — a token representing ownership in Jupiter's liquidity pool, composed of:
SOL 47% BTC 13% ETH 8% USDC 32%
JLP is redeemable at par anytime. You can always get the underlying assets back.
4
Traders Pay Fees → Yield
Jupiter's Perp Exchange lets users bet on crypto with leverage. They borrow from and trade against this pool. In exchange, they pay fees and interest — paid back to the pool as yield.
Anyone holding JLP earns yield just by holding it. $500M+ trades through this pool daily.
5
The Problem → The Hedge
JLP holds SOL, BTC, and ETH — so it moves up and down with crypto prices. That's fine if you don't mind volatility. We do.

The vault uses JLP as collateral to short BTC, SOL, and ETH in equal weight. Price up? Cancels out. Price down? Cancels out. Only the fee income remains.
An algorithm continuously rebalances the hedge as prices move, keeping the position delta-neutral at all times.
✓
What's Left: Pure Fee Income
Crypto volatility? Hedged out.
Trader fees? Kept.

The result is stable, consistent yield from market activity — not from betting on prices going up or down.
