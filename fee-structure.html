Overview

KeyVault's fee structure is built on a simple principle: we eat when you eat. Our economics are aligned with yours. We charge a modest management fee to keep the lights on, and we only earn meaningful compensation when we're generating returns above a baseline hurdle — and only on new all-time highs. If we lose money, we don't get paid until we make it back. Period.

Performance Fee Kicker

Hurdle Rate

DetailValue
Annualized Rate7.0%
Quarterly Equivalent1.75% per quarter

The hurdle rate is the minimum return threshold before any performance fees kick in. Think of it as a gate — below 7% annualized, you pay zero performance fees. Above it, fees apply to the full gain.

Why 7%? It exceeds the risk-free rate of return. You're taking risk by investing with us — the least we can do is let you keep what you could have earned in treasuries and then some. If we don't clear that bar, you pay zero performance fees.

How it works: This is a soft hurdle. Each quarter, we calculate the 1.75% hurdle. If the fund returned less than 1.75%, no performance fee is charged. If it returned more (e.g., 4%), performance fees apply to the entire 4% gain — not just the portion above the hurdle. The hurdle is a gate, not a deduction.

High Water Mark

The high water mark (HWM) ensures you never pay performance fees twice on the same dollar of gains. If the fund drops, we earn zero performance fees until we recover past the previous peak.

FAQ

When are fees charged?

Management fees and performance fees are both assessed and deducted quarterly. Crystallization occurs at each quarter-end.

What happens if I withdraw mid-quarter?

Fees are prorated to your withdrawal date. You pay the management fee for the portion of the quarter you were invested, and any accrued performance fees are crystallized at withdrawal.

Do I pay performance fees on unrealized gains?

Yes, at quarterly crystallization. Gains are marked-to-market each quarter. This protects both parties — you get a clean accounting, and the fund doesn't accumulate long periods of un-crystallized fees.

What if the fund loses money?

You pay zero performance fees. The management fee still applies (it covers operations), but no performance fee is charged until the fund exceeds its prior high water mark and clears the hurdle rate.

What's the performance fee kicker?

This is a soft hurdle structure. If the fund doesn't clear 7% annualized (1.75% per quarter), you pay zero performance fees. Once the hurdle is cleared, the 20% fee applies to the entire gain (not just the excess above the hurdle) up to the 5% quarterly kicker threshold. Above that, returns enter the kicker tier at 25%. We earn a little more when we knock it out of the park for you.

Can fees change?

Fee terms are fixed at the time of your investment and documented in your subscription agreement. Any changes require investor notification and consent.
